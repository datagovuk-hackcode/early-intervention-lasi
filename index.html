Certain issues have legs...

  • Many social issues originate in one Local Authority (LA) and impact others
  • A typical case of this is homelessness, as people in need leave to explore wealthier towns and areas of the UK looking for shelter and opportunities
  • This is a challenge for early social intervention, as there isn't a platform where LA Commissioners can exchange data illustrating these issues and orchestrate planning together
  • ... well, until today...

Enter "L.A.S.I."

  • LASI is a platform for censing intelligence / data LA Commissioners own about issues that "move", such as homelessness or domestic violence
  • It offers a view of the potential stresses that might occur within an LA and present that at a national overview level. So that no authority is seen in isolation.
  • LASI encourages collaboration and sharing between LA boundaries in developing early interventions to reduce these stress points
  • It develops a viewpoint on support and resilience across LA boundaries

The LASI stress index

  • A "stress value" is calculated for each of the Local Authorities. The index combines a number of data points from a set of various sources:

    • Census 2011
    • Shelter and other relevant non-profit open data
    • Unit Price Index
    • Spendnetwork.org.uk (existing LA spends with Charity / Social Service Providers)

The LASI stress index

  • Presented in a way to show your LA values, neighbour values and national values.
  • LA update data quarterly with projections for the next:
    • 6 months
    • 12 months
    • 18 months
    • 24 months
  • And the latest actuals for the current quarter (where data is available)

What are stress points?

For the prototype we used:

  • % of population who have not worked in last year / never worked
  • % of population leaving education without a qualification
  • % of population being reported as rough sleepers
  • % of school population that are being excluded from the main education system
  • Public health issues (teenage pregnancy, overweight population, etc)

About the calculation

  • We have taken real data and have a system to capture the projection data from the LA’s but currently the calculation is just simulated
  • In the next stages we would review the available metrics and develop a calculation formula with the relevant weightings to produce the stress index score

S.I.B.-ready

  • Able to quickly view and project stress impact on key areas of possible intervention.
  • Highlight potential areas of collaboration and cross border cooperation.
  • Visible way to show impact to potential investors.

Future Developments

  • Develop the calculation formulas
  • Research across the stakeholders on what they monitor and what areas cause stress within the authority boundaries
  • Develop practices for each Local Authority to enter data and to instigate a review process by an external ‘moderator’ to validate the data
  • Make it into a permanent forum / community for the LA Commissioners
